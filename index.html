About HeartFitML

According to the World Health Organization, heart disease is the number one leading cause of death, accounting for 17.7 millions deaths in 2015 alone. Out of all deaths caused by noncommunicable diseases, 82% occurred in low-income countries and 37% were cardiovascular based (Cardiovascular Diseases, 2017). Furthermore, only 47% of all cardiac related deaths occur outside a medical facility and only 27% of surveyed respondents were aware of symptoms that could indicate a heart issue (Heart Disease Facts, 2017). Thus, there is great demand for a cheap, accessible, effective method to diagnose quickly possible heart problems.  

Heart murmurs are irregular or intense sounds that occur during the heartbeat cycle. They can be innocent or abnormal, which may indicate an underlying heart problem. A innocent murmur usually occurs after exercise, fever, or pregnancy due to increased blood flow. However, abnormal murmurs may reveal cardiovascular disease or serious heart valve problems (Heart Murmurs 2017).

If a person is experiencing heart murmurs, we can use a simple, inexpensive device mounted to a phone to collect the heart sounds and machine learning to accurately determine if he/she is at risk of cardiovascular disease. The analysis is preliminary, meaning anyone anywhere can access it without a doctor. Given this info, the person should be advised to take action and seek specific medical treatment based on their results. Moreover, the inexpensive, mobile nature of our device directs it towards poor populations without sufficient medical attention.

The project demonstrates how machine learning and translational medical engineering can be applied to address a common, yet deadly medical problem. Future research can expand the scope of the project to cover more medical aspects.

What HeartFitML Does

HeartFitML saves lives using an inexpensive stethoscope and machine learning-based diagnosis

Inexpensive Hardware

While most stethoscopes may cost between $100 and $250, our 3D-Printed Stethoscope records the user's heartbeat to the same, if not higher, degree of accuracy in only $2.50-$5.00.

FireBase Connection

Our application, website, and machine learning database are all connected with each other with FireBase. We also store our medical database of all patients by using FireBase.

Machine Learning

At the core of our device is the Machine Learning. By using a support vector machine, keras, and sci-kit learn, we are able to create multiple convolutions and continuously increase our accuracy. The current accuracy is about 90%.

Statistics/Analysis

Our website connects with our machine learning model to accurately display the percent accuracy as well as the specific convolutions applied to our model. As a result, a database of all medical patients is also created for each doctor, as an embedded system.

Diagnosis

Our software creates a diagnosis by running the audio file through our convolutional neural network. The diagnosis is sent via our firebase connection to the mobile app, where the patient can check their diagnosis. In addition, medical professionals for the patient may access the specific data to further analyze the results, by creating an account in the website.

Alert/Contact

Once the diagnosis has been computed by our wireless technology, our app automatically sends a text message/phone call to a trusted professional doctor that had been entered earlier into the mobile app.

Our System

Software/Hardware Components

We decided to create a cheap and affordable stethoscope as opposed to stethoscopes with very high prices, such as one of the best selling models, which prices at $107. Instead, our version of the stethoscope is just as effective, for only about $5. This allows us to reach various populations and helps us to target people with lower income. For our stethoscope, the head will be applied to the patient’s chest, and the phone will record the heartbeat in the mobile application that we have designed.

To complement our device, we created a mobile application, designed in Android Studio. This application serves several purposes. First, when connected to the microphone supplied in the hardware, the app records the sound of the heartbeat for 10 seconds. Once the sound has been recorded, the “send” button sends the recorded sound to our team’s database. The patient may upload however many sound files, .wav format, they may like. Once the sound file has been processed by our machine learning database and a diagnosis has been returned, the patient will be notified within the app. If the diagnosis yields that there is indeed a heart arrhythmia, then the patient will be notified as soon as possible and an automatic text message will be sent to the patient’s doctor, to minimize any time spent in getting help from a medical professional.
